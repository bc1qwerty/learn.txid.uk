## Key Content

This book explains from the Austrian School perspective how central bank credit expansion creates cycles of economic booms and busts. Focusing on the theories of Mises and Hayek, it analyzes the mechanism by which artificially low interest rates distort the capital structure.

## Points to Consider

- One can understand that the cause of business cycles is not market failure but government monetary intervention
- The process by which artificial credit expansion triggers malinvestment is the core concept
- This theory can be used to analyze the 2008 financial crisis and current quantitative easing policies
