## Main Content

This book critically analyzes the history and structure of the U.S. Federal Reserve System (Fed), arguing that the central banking system is the fundamental cause of business cycles and economic crises. Based on Austrian School economics' theory of business cycles (ABCT), it advocates for the abolition of the Fed.

## Points to Consider

- Clearly explains how artificial interest rate manipulation by central banks induces misguided investments
- Prompts reflection on the impact of monetary issuance monopoly on democracy and individual freedom
- Ron Paul's perspective in predicting the 2008 financial crisis becomes increasingly persuasive
