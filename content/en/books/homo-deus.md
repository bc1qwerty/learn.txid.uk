## Key Content

A sequel to Sapiens, this work explores humanity's future. After overcoming famine, plague, and war, humans pursue new goals—immortality, happiness, and divinity—and the author predicts the changes that will follow. The book discusses how AI, biotechnology, and Dataism could transform human nature itself.

## Points to Consider

- The provocative argument that Dataism, a new ideology, could replace religion and humanism
- Raises the question of what "free will" means in an era when algorithms understand humans better than they understand themselves
- The warning that technological advancement may benefit only a small elite rather than all of humanity resonates deeply
