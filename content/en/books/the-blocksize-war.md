## Key Content

A detailed account of Bitcoin's most intense internal conflict, the blocksize debate. This book covers the period from 2015 to 2017 when the Bitcoin community was divided over block size expansion versus the status quo, the activation of SegWit, and the subsequent Bitcoin Cash (BCH) hard fork.

## Points to Consider

- Understand how Bitcoin's decentralized governance actually works in practice
- See how consensus among node operators (users) overcame the influence of miners and corporations
- Learn from the case where individual sovereignty protected the Bitcoin protocol through UASF (User Activated Soft Fork)
- Realize that Bitcoin is not merely a technology but a system of social consensus
