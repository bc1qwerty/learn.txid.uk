## What is Time Preference?

Imagine a single chocolate bar placed in front of you. You can eat it right now, or receive two bars a year from now. Which would you choose? This choice reveals your 'time preference.'

Time preference refers to the preference between present consumption and future consumption. A high time preference means you choose the present, while a low time preference means you willingly wait for the future. Austrian economists believe this concept goes beyond simple personal taste—it is a key factor that determines the level of civilization's advancement.

## How Low Time Preference Creates Civilization

Consider a farmer planting seeds. He buries grain that he could eat right now, waiting months before harvest. This is clearly an action of low time preference. All progress—savings and investment, education, technological development—is created by people who sacrifice the present for the future.

Looking at history, societies with low time preference flourished. Investing 100 years in building cathedrals, pouring decades into children's education, dedicating lifetimes to fundamental science—this culture ultimately created abundance. Conversely, high time preference societies focus on short-term pleasure and consume the present by mortgaging the future.

## Why Declining Currency Value Distorts Time Preference

After 1971, global currencies lost their link to gold. Governments and central banks gained the ability to print money at will. The result: persistent inflation. Your savings lose value every year.

In such an environment, what is the rational choice? Not saving, but debt. Since future money will be worth less than today's, you should borrow as much as possible now and repay later with devalued currency. Not just individuals, but governments and corporations act this way.

Inflation artificially raises society's overall time preference. The virtue of 'sacrificing today for tomorrow' becomes 'foolishness.' Savers lose out while borrowers are rewarded in this system.

## Bitcoin Reverses Time Preference

Bitcoin's supply is fixed at 21 million coins. No one can create more. This is the most certain scarcity in history. As time passes, those holding Bitcoin are likely to have increasing purchasing power.

Holding Bitcoin is an act of deferring present consumption and choosing the future. A recovery of low time preference. When Bitcoin holders cry 'HODL' (long-term holding), it's not merely an investment strategy. It is the natural savings culture created by sound money.

Historically, the same phenomenon existed during the gold standard era. People collected gold coins, and they became a safety net for the future. It was saving, not speculation. Bitcoin is the gold standard for the digital age.

## Returning to the Foundation of Civilization

Low time preference is both an individual virtue and society's capital. The more people who think long-term, the more sustainable development a society achieves. Bitcoin is a tool that revives this virtue.

Government-printed money distorts our time preference and forces short-term thinking. Bitcoin points in the opposite direction. Scarce, incorruptible, and preserving value as time passes.

Holding Bitcoin is not simply 'hoping for price increases.' It is choosing future stability over present pleasure, choosing savings over debt, choosing capital accumulation over consumption. And that is where true prosperity begins.
