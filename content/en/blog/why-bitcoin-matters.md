## Why Money Matters

Money is not simply a medium of exchange. Money is the vessel in which we store the value of our time and labor. A good vessel preserves its contents; a bad vessel lets them slowly leak away.

Since Nixon abolished the gold standard in 1971, all [fiat money](/ideas/fiat-money/) exists only on the promise of governments. And those promises have been broken continuously. The dollar's purchasing power has fallen roughly 87% over the past 50 years.

## What Makes Bitcoin Different

Bitcoin differs from existing money in three fundamental properties.

**Supply cap**: 21 million. This number is written into the code, and no central bank, no government can change it. No monetary asset in human history has possessed such absolute scarcity.

**Censorship resistance**: Bitcoin transactions require no permission from any third party. With an internet connection, you can send value to anyone in the world, at any time.

**Self-sovereignty**: Bitcoin is controlled only by whoever holds the private keys. Bank account freezes, asset seizures, capital controls — genuine property rights free from all of these become possible.

## Not an Investment Asset — An Exit

Many people see Bitcoin as "the next asset to go up." But Bitcoin's true value lies not in price appreciation, but in the fact that it is an exit from the state's monopoly on money.

In the fiat money system, your savings are eroded every year by the invisible tax of inflation. Bitcoin is history's first technological answer — a way to escape this structural plunder.

Understanding Bitcoin is not simply understanding a technology. It is asking the fundamental question of what money is, and what the relationship between the state and the individual ought to be.
