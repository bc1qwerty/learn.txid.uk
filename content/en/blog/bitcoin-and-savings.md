## The Crisis of Saving

Our parents' generation put money in the bank and earned interest. Because bank interest rates exceeded inflation, savings grew in real terms.

Today is different. Decades of low-interest-rate policy have brought bank deposit interest rates to nearly zero. Meanwhile, real inflation is often higher than official figures. Leaving money in the bank is, in effect, losing a little each year.

Who benefits from this structure? Those who hold assets like real estate and stocks. When inflation erodes the value of cash, asset prices nominally rise. This is the mechanism that widens the wealth gap.

## Viewing Bitcoin as a Savings Vehicle

Bitcoin is one option for escaping this structure.

**Fixed supply**: Bitcoin cannot exceed 21 million. Because fewer new bitcoins are issued over time, demand staying constant means prices face structural upward pressure.

**Low storage cost**: Storing gold requires a safe or custodial service. Real estate incurs taxes, maintenance fees, and repair costs. Bitcoin is accessible anywhere just by memorizing a 12-word seed phrase.

**Divisibility**: Divisible down to 1 satoshi (0.00000001 BTC), making it easy to save even small amounts.

## Dollar-Cost Averaging

The most representative strategy for using Bitcoin as a savings vehicle is **Dollar-Cost Averaging (DCA)** — purchasing a fixed amount at regular intervals regardless of price.

Automatically buying a fixed amount weekly or monthly means buying less when prices are high and more when they are low, which lowers the average purchase price over time.

## Cautions

Bitcoin is highly volatile. In the short term, prices have dropped by more than 50%. If you treat Bitcoin as a savings vehicle, a long-term horizon of at least four years is necessary.

You should also only invest within your means. Bitcoin can be an alternative to traditional savings, but you should be cautious about holding even your living expenses or emergency funds in Bitcoin.
