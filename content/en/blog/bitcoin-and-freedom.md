## Money Is Freedom

Freedom is not an abstract concept. Freedom is a set of concrete abilities — the freedom to live where you want, to hold the job you want, to spend money on what you want.

And at the foundation of all these freedoms lies **economic freedom**. If you cannot control the money you earn, your other freedoms are also practically constrained.

The modern financial system looks free on the surface, but it is a structure in which states can limit economic freedom in many ways.

## How States Control Money

**Capital controls**: Many countries impose strict limits on holding foreign currency or sending money abroad. In China, Argentina, Venezuela, and elsewhere, citizens cannot use their money freely.

**Account freezes**: Governments or banks can in some cases freeze accounts without a court order. In Canada in 2022, the accounts of truckers' protest participants were frozen by government order.

**Inflation**: A continuous, invisible mechanism that reduces the purchasing power of those who hold savings.

**Payment censorship**: Payments to certain sites or services can be refused. WikiLeaks faced a crisis in 2010 when PayPal, Visa, and Mastercard blocked payments to it.

## The Freedom Bitcoin Provides

Bitcoin is a technology that responds to these control mechanisms.

**Permissionless transactions**: No one's approval is required. With an internet connection, you can send value anywhere in the world.

**Unseizable property**: If you hold your own private keys, no government can take your Bitcoin.

**Censorship resistance**: Blocking a specific address's transactions at the network level is practically impossible.

**Borderless movement**: Bitcoin crosses borders without a single cent requiring customs declaration.

## Technology for Freedom

Bitcoin is not a political tool. It takes no side on ideology. But the functions it provides — permissionless transactions, censorship resistance, non-confiscability — technically guarantee individual economic freedom. This is why Bitcoin goes beyond being a mere investment asset.
