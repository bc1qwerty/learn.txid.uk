## Gold's Legacy

Gold has been humanity's store of value for thousands of years. Why gold? Because it is scarce, does not corrode, is difficult to counterfeit, and can be divided. These properties placed gold on the throne of money.

In most civilizations before the 20th century, money was gold, or was backed by gold. Under the gold standard, governments could not issue currency beyond their gold reserves. This enforced fiscal discipline.

## Why Bitcoin Is Superior to Gold

Bitcoin digitally replicates all of gold's advantages while adding properties gold lacks.

**Stronger scarcity**: The total quantity of gold is unknowable. New mines are discovered, and asteroid mining could one day increase supply. Bitcoin's supply cap is precisely 21 million, written into the code. This is mathematically guaranteed scarcity.

**Verifiability**: Confirming gold's purity requires specialized equipment. There have even been fraud cases involving tungsten coated in gold. Bitcoin's authenticity can be instantly verified by anyone with software.

**Portability**: Transporting $100 million worth of gold across a border is physically near-impossible. The same value in Bitcoin can be carried in your head by memorizing 12 words (a seed phrase).

**Divisibility**: Dividing gold into 0.00001 grams is not practical. Bitcoin is divisible down to 1 satoshi (0.00000001 BTC).

## Where Gold Still Has an Edge

Gold has thousands of years of verified history. It exists without electricity, without the internet. It has a physical form that can be stored in ways that are difficult to confiscate.

Bitcoin is only 15 years old. Some still view it as being in an early experimental stage.

## Digital Gold

Many people call Bitcoin "digital gold." This is not merely a metaphor. Bitcoin is a new technology that performs the role gold has played — preserving value across time — more efficiently and with greater censorship resistance.

Gold was the store of value for the physical world. Bitcoin is its equivalent for the digital world.
