## How Is It Different from Mainstream Economics?

Most of what universities teach today is grounded in Keynesianism — the premise that governments must actively intervene in the economy to manage the business cycle.

The Austrian School rejects this premise at its root. Economic phenomena originate in the actions of individual human beings, and the [spontaneous order](/ideas/spontaneous-order/) of markets is more efficient than any central plan.

## Core Concepts

### Subjective Theory of Value

The value of a good does not reside in the good itself but is determined by the subjective judgment of the individual using it. This is why a glass of water in the desert is worth more than a glass of water beside a lake. Carl Menger established this principle in 1871, and it transformed the paradigm of economics.

### Time Preference

Humans prefer present goods to future goods. The degree of this [time preference](/ideas/time-preference/) determines interest rates, and beyond that, the level of civilization's development. A society with low time preference saves and invests; a society with high time preference focuses on consumption.

### Austrian Business Cycle Theory (ABCT)

This theory holds that the artificial lowering of interest rates by central banks is the root cause of boom-and-bust cycles. Developed by Mises and Hayek, it remains one of the clearest frameworks for explaining the 2008 financial crisis.

### The Economic Calculation Problem

In a socialist system, rational economic calculation is impossible because market prices do not exist. Mises posed this problem in 1920, and it was historically validated by the collapse of the Soviet Union.

## Why Austrian Economics Now?

Quantitative easing, zero interest rates, exploding government debt — the problems of the modern economy are exactly what the Austrian School warned about 100 years ago. To truly understand Bitcoin, you must first understand why [sound money](/ideas/sound-money/) matters. And that understanding begins with Austrian Economics.
