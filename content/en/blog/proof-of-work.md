## Reaching Consensus Without Trust

In a distributed system, is it possible to make all participants agree on the same truth without trusting each other? This is the core problem Bitcoin solved.

Before Bitcoin, this was considered impossible. There was no way to get everyone to agree on "who owns what" without a central authority. Satoshi Nakamoto solved this problem with **Proof of Work (PoW)**.

## What Is Proof of Work

The principle of Proof of Work is simple: to add a new block, you must prove an enormous amount of computational work.

Miners repeat billions of random calculations to find a hash value satisfying specific conditions. The process is like rolling dice countless times until a specific number combination appears.

Finding the hash value is difficult, but verifying that it is correct takes only an instant. This asymmetry is the key.

## Why Proof of Work Matters

**Cost of attack**: To attack the Bitcoin network and manipulate transactions, you need more than 51% of the total network hash rate. This requires billions of dollars in hardware and enormous electricity costs. The cost of attack exceeds the potential gain.

**Immutability of history**: To change a block that has already been recorded, you must redo the Proof of Work for every subsequent block. The longer the chain grows, the exponentially harder it becomes to revise the past.

**Decentralization**: Tens of thousands of miners reach consensus through competition, without any central authority.

## Criticism of Energy Consumption

The biggest criticism of Proof of Work is energy consumption. The Bitcoin network consumes electricity at national scale.

Bitcoin proponents respond as follows: this energy consumption physically underpins the network's security. Also, because miners seek the cheapest electricity for profitability, they actively utilize renewable energy and surplus power.

Proof of Work is not merely a technical choice. It is a fundamental innovation that makes a decentralized digital currency possible — one that operates without trust.
