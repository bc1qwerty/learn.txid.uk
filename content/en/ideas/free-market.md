**Free Market** is an economic system in which prices and production are determined by the voluntary exchange of individuals without government intervention.

In a free market, prices are naturally formed through the interaction of supply and demand. This price signal serves as an information system that allocates scarce resources to where they are most needed.

Core principles:
- **Voluntary exchange**: All transactions are concluded because they benefit both parties
- **Price signals**: Prices aggregate and communicate dispersed information
- **Competition**: Competition to provide consumers with better value drives innovation

Hayek explained that central planning fails because it cannot gather dispersed knowledge in one place. Bitcoin's fee market is an example of free market principles operating in digital space.
