**Private Property Rights** are the rights of individuals to exclusively use, dispose of, and exchange their property.

According to John Locke's logic, property rights are established as follows:
1. You own your own body (self-ownership)
2. You own your own labor
3. You own natural resources mixed with your labor (original appropriation)
4. Ownership transfers through voluntary exchange

Why private property rights are important:
- They create incentive structures that enable efficient allocation of resources
- They serve as the material foundation protecting individual freedom from state power
- They are a prerequisite for economic calculation (price formation)

Private keys in Bitcoin technically guarantee private property rights in the digital world. Only the person holding the key can move funds, and no one can seize or freeze them.
