**Minarchism** is the position that the state's role should be limited to the minimum functions necessary to protect individual rights—police, courts, and national defense.

Systematized by Robert Nozick in *Anarchy, State, and Utopia*, this position is distinguished within libertarianism from anarcho-capitalism:

| | Minarchism | Anarcho-capitalism |
|---|---|---|
| State | Minimally necessary | Unnecessary |
| Monopoly on force | Limited to rights protection | Private competition |
| Taxation | Minimally acceptable | Completely rejected |

Core arguments of minarchism:
- Markets provide most services more efficiently than the state
- Education, healthcare, pensions, and other services should be provided by the private sector
- Regulation protects existing businesses and harms consumers

Bitcoin is an example of replacing a state function—currency issuance—with technology, serving as a practical tool of minarchism.
