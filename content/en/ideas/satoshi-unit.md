**Satoshi (sat)** is the smallest unit of bitcoin, where 1 BTC = 100 million satoshis (100,000,000 sats).

Named after Satoshi Nakamoto, the founder of bitcoin, all amounts in the bitcoin protocol are internally processed in satoshi units. Because it uses integer arithmetic rather than decimal points, floating-point errors do not occur.

As the price of bitcoin rises, the sat unit becomes more intuitive than the BTC unit for everyday transactions. For example, a cup of coffee is much easier to read as 5,000 sats than as 0.00005 BTC.

> "1 sat = 1 sat" — An expression used by bitcoiners to convey their determination not to be shaken by fluctuations in fiat currency prices.
