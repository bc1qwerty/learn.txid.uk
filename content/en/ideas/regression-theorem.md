**The Regression Theorem of Money** is a theory presented by Mises, which argues that the value of all money can be traced back through past exchange rates to its origin in non-monetary uses (value as a commodity).

The logical flow:
1. The reason we accept money today is because someone else accepted it yesterday
2. The reason it was accepted yesterday is because it was accepted the day before
3. Tracing back this way, we eventually reach a point when the money had value not as money, but as a commodity
4. Gold originated from non-monetary value such as jewelry and industrial use, and later became money

Bitcoin and the Regression Theorem:
- Critics: Bitcoin has no non-monetary use, so it cannot become money
- Counterargument: Bitcoin's initial value originated from the **service** of censorship-resistant P2P transmission. The Regression Theorem does not require only physical commodities
- In fact, Bitcoin went through this process as it formed prices between 2009-2010 starting from zero
