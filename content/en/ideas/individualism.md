**Methodological Individualism** is the principle that all social phenomena should ultimately be explained by reducing them to the actions, choices, and value judgments of individuals.

This is not an ethical claim but an analytical methodology:
- "The state decided" → In reality, specific bureaucrats made the decision
- "The market wants this" → In reality, the preferences of millions of individuals are reflected
- "Society demands it" → In reality, specific individuals are making these claims

Why this principle matters:
- It prevents the error of attributing will or purpose to collectives
- It provides a critical perspective against infringing on individual rights in the name of "the public good"
- It makes us confront the reality of individual lives hidden behind aggregate economic variables (GDP, price indices)

Bitcoin implements methodological individualism as technology. Each node independently validates the rules, and the system is maintained not by a collective entity called "the network," but by the consensus of individual nodes.
